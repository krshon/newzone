RBI Proposes Delay Mechanism for High-Value Digital Payments to Reduce Online Fraud

April 2026 · Reserve Bank Policy Desk · 3 min read

Left: RBI headquarters in Mumbai. Right: Growth of digital payments across India.

The Reserve Bank of India has proposed introducing a short delay window for high-value digital transactions above ₹10,000 to curb rising online fraud. The proposal would allow users time to cancel suspicious transfers while strengthening safeguards in India’s fast-growing real-time payments ecosystem.

The Reserve Bank of India has proposed a new safeguard mechanism that could introduce a short delay before processing certain high-value digital payments, aiming to reduce fraud risks in India’s rapidly expanding online transactions ecosystem.

Officials said the measure would primarily apply to first-time transactions between accounts or unusually large transfers exceeding ₹10,000. The delay window would allow users to verify payment authenticity and cancel suspicious activity before completion.

India currently processes billions of Unified Payments Interface (UPI) transactions each month, making it one of the world’s fastest-growing digital payment networks. However, authorities have warned that fraud attempts have increased alongside adoption.

If implemented, the proposal could balance convenience with security while preserving the speed advantages that made India’s digital payment infrastructure globally influential.
