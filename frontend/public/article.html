RBI Proposes Delay Mechanism for High-Value Digital Payments to Reduce Online Fraud

April 2026 · Reserve Bank Policy Desk · 3 min read

Left: RBI headquarters in Mumbai. Right: Growth of digital payments across India.

The Reserve Bank of India has proposed introducing a short delay window for high-value digital transactions above ₹10,000 to curb rising online fraud.

The Reserve Bank of India has proposed a safeguard mechanism introducing a short delay before processing certain high-value digital payments.

The delay would apply to first-time transfers or transactions exceeding ₹10,000.

India processes billions of UPI transactions monthly, though fraud attempts have risen alongside adoption.

The proposal aims to balance security with speed advantages in India’s digital payments infrastructure.
