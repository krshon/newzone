📋 RBI Monetary Policy — April 2025 Deep Briefing

The Decision: The Reserve Bank of India's Monetary Policy Committee voted 4-2 to hold the repo rate at 6.5% for the eighth consecutive meeting, maintaining its stance of "withdrawal of accommodation" even as global central banks signal pivot timelines.

Why It Matters: India's CPI inflation remained sticky at 5.1% in March, driven by food prices — particularly pulses and vegetables — keeping the MPC cautious. Core inflation, however, dipped to 3.4%, the lowest since 2020.

Market Reaction: Sensex rallied 412 points as traders interpreted the hold as "rate cut priced in for Q2." 10-year G-sec yields softened to 7.02%. Banking stocks outperformed with HDFC Bank and SBI leading gains.

RBI Rate Decision — AI Deep Briefing
⬡ INTERACTIVE INTELLIGENCE BRIEFING · SYNTHESIZED FROM 8 ET ARTICLES

The Decision: The Reserve Bank of India held the repo rate at 6.5%. The MPC's 4-2 vote reflects a split committee navigating a tricky path between food-driven inflation (5.1%) and slowing private consumption growth.

Key Insight: Governor Das emphasized the "last mile of disinflation" requires patience — a phrase that markets interpreted as rate cuts deferred to H2 2025. Bond markets rallied anyway, pricing in June probability at 64%.

Contrarian View: HSBC and Nomura both see a risk of overtightening. With private capex stalling and rural wages flat in real terms, they argue a cut sooner could reignite consumption without meaningfully reigniting inflation.
